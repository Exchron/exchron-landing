Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about Exchron and exoplanet classification

Our ML algorithms use deep neural networks trained on NASA's Kepler and TESS mission data. The system analyzes transit light curves - the dimming patterns when a planet passes in front of its star. We employ convolutional neural networks (CNNs) to detect these subtle patterns and classify them as planetary signals or false positives with 97.3% accuracy.

You can upload various formats including FITS files from space telescopes, CSV files with time-series photometry data, and JSON formatted light curves. Our platform supports data from Kepler, TESS, and ground-based surveys. We also provide pre-processed datasets from NASA archives for training and testing your models.
